\section*{Model}

Tick-counter coupled model under PDEVS:
\[
  C = \langle X_C,\, Y_C,\, D,\, \{M_d\},\, \text{EIC},\, \text{EOC},\, \text{IC} \rangle
\]
\[
  D = \{G_{1/10},\; G_{1},\; K\},\quad X_C = \emptyset,\quad Y_C \subseteq \mathbb{N}
\]
\[
  \text{IC} = \{(G_{1/10} \to K),\; (G_{1} \to K)\}
  \qquad
  \text{EOC} = \{(K \to C.\texttt{out})\}
\]

Expected invariant: every output of $K$ equals $10$.

\subsection*{Atomic model definitions}

Generator $G_p$:
\[
  ta_{G}(s) = p,\quad \delta^G_{\text{int}}(s) = s,\quad \lambda_G(s) = \mathit{out}
\]

Counter $K$ ($S_K = \mathbb{N} \times \{\text{idle},\, \text{ready}\}$):
\begin{align*}
  ta_K(n,\text{idle})  &= \infty \qquad ta_K(n,\text{ready}) = 0 \\
  \delta^K_{\text{ext}}(s,e,x) &=
    \begin{cases}
      (n + |x|,\; \text{idle})  & \forall m \in x: m \neq 0 \\
      (n,\;       \text{ready}) & \exists m \in x: m = 0
    \end{cases} \\
  \delta^K_{\text{int}}(n,\text{ready}) &= (0,\, \text{idle}) \\
  \lambda_K(n,\text{ready})             &= n
\end{align*}

Confluence (internal-first):
$\delta^K_{\text{con}}(s, e, x) = \delta^K_{\text{ext}}(\delta^K_{\text{int}}(s), 0, x)$

\subsection*{Message encoding}

CDBoost uses value-based coupling: $G_{1/10}$ outputs $1$ (tick) and $G_1$
outputs $0$ (reset).  Both route to $K$ via IC.  $K$ distinguishes the two
signals by value:  any non-zero message increments the count; a zero message
triggers output and reset.  This matches the \texttt{infinite\_counter} model
shipped with CDBoost.

\section*{Implementation Notes}

\paragraph{Counter $K$.}
\texttt{cdboost::pdevs::basic\_models::infinite\_counter<TIME, std::any>},
wrapped via \texttt{k\_counter.hpp}.

\paragraph{Generators.}
\texttt{cdboost::pdevs::basic\_models::generator<TIME, std::any>},
instantiated by factory functions in \texttt{tick\_gen.hpp} and
\texttt{reset\_gen.hpp}.  Periods are passed as constructor arguments to
allow both \texttt{double} and \texttt{boost::rational<int>} callers.

\paragraph{Simulation driver.}
Output is collected by calling \texttt{coordinator::collectOutputs(t)}
before each \texttt{advanceSimulation(t)}.  Termination is by reset count,
not by float end-time, to keep the float and rational runs comparable.

\paragraph{TIME types exercised.} \texttt{double} and
\texttt{boost::rational<int>} (see expectations below).

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section*{Expected Observations}

\textbf{Double time:} IEEE~754 cannot represent $1/10$ exactly.
Accumulated error over repeated addition of \texttt{0.1} shifts occasional
$G_{1/10}$ ticks across a 1-second boundary; $K$ outputs $9$ or $11$.

\textbf{Rational time:} $G_{1/10}$ and $G_1$ are always simultaneously
imminent at integer seconds; confluence correctly resolves the tie and $K$
always outputs $10$.
